About Steganography

Steganography is the science of hiding a message so that only the sender and receiver of the message know or suspect that it is actually a message. It has been used since the time of the Ancient Greeks and that is where its name originated from. Two Greek words steganos and graphei which means concealed writing. It is completely different to cryptography where the message is encrypted and may draw suspicion. Instead the message is concealed to hide its existence and thus draw no suspicion while reducing the chance of it being detected. It is common that both Cryptography and Steganography be used together to significantly improve protecting information.

There are a number of methods used in Stenography to hide a message, which include the following:
  • Writing messages in invisible ink to conceal information.
  • Concealing information using character arrangement in a message.
  • Using microdots to conceal information.
  • Using paintings or drawings to conceal information.

People have been using Steganography throughout history to hide their message such as in World War II when countries used it to keep the enemy from reading important messages. Some were developed by putting a new twist on old methods. But with the arrival of computers more methods have been developed such as manipulating pixels to conceal information.
